Add ODSL Top Times to Your Team Website

A step-by-step guide for SwimTopia team sites. About five minutes, no technical skills needed.

A quick, friendly note — this isn't an official ODSL product. We built it for our own team, put it on the Willowsford North Waves site (you can see it live here), and the reception was great, so we wanted to share it with other teams who might enjoy it too. It's built entirely from the top-times CSV files the league posts after each meet (usually Monday and Friday mornings). When ODSL releases the 2026 All-Star time standards, we update them in one place and the new cut lines appear on every team's embedded page automatically — there's nothing for your team to maintain.

This guide shows you how to put that same rankings page on your own team's site. It's a single interactive page that refreshes after every meet: visitors filter by gender, age group, and event and can pick any team — and you can set your team as the default so your swimmers are highlighted the moment it loads. You never touch the data; it updates for everyone from one shared source.

Part 1 Get your team's embed code

Find your team below and click Copy. That copies a ready-to-paste snippet with your team already set as the default.

Tip: Your copied code includes ?team=YOURCODE. That highlights your swimmers and turns on an “Only [your team]” toggle by default — but visitors can still switch to any team in the league.

Part 2 Publish it on your SwimTopia site

1

Sign in and open Manage Team

Sign in to your SwimTopia site as a team admin. In the top-right corner click Welcome [Your Name], then choose Manage Team.

2

Go to Website

In the blue admin menu, click Website.

3

Click New Page

On the Pages tab, click the green New Page button (lower right).

4

Name the page and set it to Full Width

Give it a Title (for example ODSL Rankings). Set Page Template to Full Width so the table has room to breathe. Check Published? when you're ready for visitors to see it, then click Save. (Leave “Hide From Navigation” unchecked if you want it to appear in your top menu.)

5

Open the page and click Edit Content

Find your new page in the list and click Edit Content on the right.

6

Add a Text Area snippet

Click Add Snippet, then drag Text Area from the palette into the gray content area.

7

Switch the editor to HTML

In the Text Area toolbar, click the </> (HTML) button on the far right. The box turns dark — that means you're in HTML mode.

8

Paste your embed code and click Create

Paste the embed code you copied in Part 1 into the dark box, then click Create. Click Exit when you're done — and that's it.

After it's live: nothing to maintain. The page is rebuilt from each meet's official results (typically Monday and Friday mornings), and your embedded copy shows the new numbers automatically. The 2026 All-Star cut lines drop in for everyone the moment they're published.
