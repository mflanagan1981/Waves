How the projection works — and what it leaves out

Data is the season composite. Every weekly top-times export is unioned and each swimmer is seeded at their best time per event, then events are re-ranked. That reaches well past any single week's top 30, and it deepens every time a new meet file is added.

Each team's lineup is optimized for margin. ODSL lets a swimmer enter three individual events; the engine assigns each swimmer the three events that add the most to their team's point margin, given who else is in the water. Because places pay only to fifth and drop steeply (6-4-3-2-1), it spreads depth across an age group instead of stacking one event — the way a coach does. Both teams are optimized against each other and iterated until the lineups settle, so the score assumes both sides coach well.

Scoring is the ODSL dual-meet rule: 6-4-3-2-1 to the top five in individual events, no per-team cap, ties split per the rulebook. Relays score 6-4-2 to the top three.

Individual times are real season bests. Willowsford North comes from the SwimTopia report card; Willowsford South comes from four meet result files. Both are cross-checked against the league rankings.

The relay program is the dual-meet program (which differs from the championship rulebook): 8-and-Under is a 4-by-25 freestyle relay; 9-10, 11-12, and 13-14 are 4-by-25 medley relays; 15-18 is a 4-by-50 medley relay; and each gender's open relay is a 4-by-50 freestyle with one swimmer from each of 9-10, 11-12, 13-14, and 15-18. Relay lineups are built from individual swims to suggest who swims: fifty-meter legs use real times, and twenty-five-meter legs are estimated as the swimmer's fifty divided by two (which runs slightly slow versus a real twenty-five). Measured season-best squad times, where a team has them, are shown alongside the built lineup for reference.

Each team enters up to two relays, scored 6-4-2 to the top three across both teams. By default both teams stack — fastest four in the first relay, next four in the second. The relay strategy controls let you switch either team to spread, which splits those eight swimmers into two balanced relays to chase a 1-2 finish. Spread only fires where first place stays locked by at least the buffer you set, given as a percentage of the relay time; a larger buffer spreads only the relays a team dominates, a smaller buffer spreads more aggressively. Each team is set on its own, since in any one relay only the faster team can go 1-2, and every race shows both outcomes with a toggle so you can override a single race. Estimates sum flat times and do not credit the rolling-start advantage on legs two through four, which inflates both teams similarly. Relay swim-ups are not modeled.

Blind spots are seeded, not gifted. Where a team has age-group swimmers but none ranked in a given event, an estimated entry at that event's cutoff time stands in (marked est.) so a sweep against literally nobody is not assumed. Events using an estimate are flagged and shaded.

What-ifs: on the Lineups tab, click any swimmer to scratch them (absence, illness) and the whole projection re-optimizes and re-scores around it.
